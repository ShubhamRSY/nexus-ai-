Frequently asked questions

Is Nexus open source?

No. Nexus is proprietary software operated as a hosted service (Nexus Cloud). Source code is not publicly licensed for redistribution. Enterprise customers may negotiate private deployment terms.

Can I use Nexus for free?

Yes. The Free plan includes 2 AI agents, chat, email, and 5 integrations at $0/month. Voice, WhatsApp, SMS, and social channels require a paid plan.

What happens when I hit a paid feature on the Free plan?

Locked channels (e.g. Voice, WhatsApp) require a paid plan. Send an enquiry to request pricing and access.

How does billing work?

We don’t publish prices on the website. Request a quote and we’ll share plan options for your team. The Free tier is available instantly via sign-up — no card required.

Can I self-host Nexus?

Self-hosting and private cloud are available on Enterprise plans. Contact us for licensing and deployment.

How do I contact support?

Email hello@nexus.com or use the enquiry form. All messages are delivered to our team inbox.

Can I use hello@nexus.com?

Public contact is listed as hello@nexus.com. To receive mail at your own domain (e.g. @nexus.com), purchase the domain and set up email forwarding (e.g. Cloudflare Email Routing) to your inbox. Until then, use the contact link above.

Where is my data stored?

Nexus Cloud runs on your operator's infrastructure (e.g. PostgreSQL, Redis, encrypted integration vault). See our Privacy Policy for details.
